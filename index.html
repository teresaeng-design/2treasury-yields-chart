Is the 2-year yield more "accurate" than the Fed Funds Rate?
They measure different things. The FFR is the current policy rate. The 2-year yield is a market forecast of where rates will average over the next two years.

Across 12 detected stability windows, the 2-year diverged and moved ahead of the FFR by 1–12 months. Longest lead: 12 months before GFC cuts (2006). Most recent: 9 months before the first 2024 cut. Predictive, not more accurate
What does the spread (2yr − FFR) indicate?
Positive spread (green): Markets price in future hikes — 2yr trades above current FFR.
Negative / inverted (red): Markets price in cuts. Has preceded every easing cycle in this data.

Deepest inversions: −1.46% (Jan 2008, GFC) and −1.51% (Sep 2024, post-pandemic). Widest positive: +2.21% (Apr 2022, when the 2yr surged well ahead of a Fed still at zero). Deep inversion = easing cycle signal
Major financial & world events (2006–2026)
2007–09: GFC — subprime collapse, Lehman, TARP, ZLB.
2010–12: European sovereign debt crisis, US credit downgrade.
2013: Taper Tantrum — Bernanke tapering signal spikes yields.
2015–18: First post-GFC normalisation (0%→2.5%).
2020: COVID — 150bps emergency cuts in two weeks.
2022–23: Fastest hiking cycle in 40 years; worst bond drawdown on record.
2025–26: Tariff shock; easing continues. Annotated on chart below
